29 /*
30  EvaluationContext has two main purposes:
31 
32  1. It packages up the information that heuristics and open lists
33  need in order to perform an evaluation: the state, the g value of
34  the node, and whether it was reached by a preferred operator.
35 
36  2. It caches computed heuristic values and preferred operators for
37  the current evaluation so that they do not need to be computed
38  multiple times just because they appear in multiple contexts,
39  and also so that we don't need to know a priori which heuristics
40  need to be evaluated throughout the evaluation process.
41 
42  For example, our current implementation of A* search uses the
43  heuristic value h at least three times: twice for its
44  tie-breaking open list based on <g + h, h> and a third time for
45  its "progress evaluator" that produces output whenever we reach a
46  new best f value.
47 */
